# How to Find Professor Office Hours

*Last Updated: January 29, 2026*

Quick guide to finding your professor's office hours and getting help.

---

## üìç **Where to Find Office Hours**

### 1. **Course Syllabus** (Most Common)
- **Check:** First day handout or Canvas syllabus
- **Look for:** "Office Hours" section
- **Usually includes:** Days, times, location (office room or Zoom link)

> **Tip:** Save your syllabus! It's your #1 resource all semester.

---

### 2. **Canvas Course Page**
- Log into Canvas
- Go to your course
- Check:
  - Course homepage
  - "Syllabus" tab
  - "Home" module
  - Professor's contact info section

---

### 3. **Professor's Email**
- **First week email:** Professors often send welcome email with office hours
- **Email signature:** Many professors include hours in their signature
- **Ask directly:** "Could you share your office hours for this semester?"

---

### 4. **Department Website**
- Go to your department website
  - Example: ramapo.edu/psych (Psychology)
  - Example: ramapo.edu/business (Business)
- Find "Faculty" or "People" page
- Click on your professor's profile
- Some (not all) list office hours

---

### 5. **Professor's Office Door**
- Visit their office in person
- Most professors post hours on their door
- **Office locations:** Check syllabus or department office

---

## üí° **Office Hours Tips**

### **What Are Office Hours?**
- Dedicated time for student questions and help
- **Free!** No appointment needed (usually)
- Can discuss:
  - Homework questions
  - Exam review
  - Paper feedback
  - Course concepts
  - General advice

### **When to Go:**
‚úÖ **Before exams** - Review material  
‚úÖ **After getting an assignment back** - Understand mistakes  
‚úÖ **When stuck on homework** - Get guidance  
‚úÖ **Early in semester** - Introduce yourself  
‚úÖ **Before dropping** - Discuss struggles  

### **Don't Wait Until You're Failing!**
- Office hours are for **everyone**
- Professors want to help
- Going early builds relationships

---

## üè¢ **Common Office Hour Formats**

### **In-Person**
- Visit professor's office
- Bring materials (notes, textbook, assignment)
- Come prepared with specific questions

### **Virtual (Zoom)**
- Link usually in Canvas or email
- Same as in-person, but online
- Make sure camera/mic work

### **By Appointment**
- Some professors prefer scheduling
- Email to set up a time
- Check Canvas for booking system (some use Calendly)

### **Drop-In**
- No appointment needed
- First-come, first-served
- May wait if others are there

## üìÖ **Typical Office Hours**

Most professors offer:
- **2-4 hours per week**
- **Before or after class** (sometimes)
- **Mix of in-person and virtual**

### Common Times:
- Tuesday/Thursday afternoons
- Monday/Wednesday mornings
- Friday mornings
- By appointment

## üÜò **Can't Make Office Hours?**

### **Options:**

1. **Email for appointment**
   - "I can't make your regular hours. Could we meet at another time?"
   - Suggest 2-3 times you're available

2. **Ask via email**
   - Keep it brief and specific
   - Professors may answer quick questions by email

3. **Use tutoring services**
   - Learning Commons tutoring
   - Supplemental Instruction (SI) sessions
   - Writing Center

4. **Form study groups**
   - Study with classmates
   - Teach each other

## ‚ùì **Common Questions**

**Q: Do I need an appointment?**  
A: Usually no, unless professor specifies "by appointment only"

**Q: Can I bring a friend?**  
A: Yes! Study buddies welcome (unless it's about a grade)

**Q: What if no one else is there?**  
A: Great! You get one-on-one time with professor

**Q: Is it awkward?**  
A: No! Professors hold office hours specifically to help students

**Q: Will they think I'm dumb?**  
A: Absolutely not. Asking questions shows you care about learning

**Q: Can I go if I'm doing well?**  
A: YES! Go to deepen understanding or ask for research opportunities
